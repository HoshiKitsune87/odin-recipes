Japanese Ramen

The Ramen Dish

The ramen dish is a Japanese noodle soup with a combination of rich flavoured broth, one variety of types of noodle and a selection of meats or vegetables are often topped with a boiled egg.

Ingredients consist of:

  • 700ml chicken stock
  • 3 garlic
  • 4 tbsp soy sauce, plus extra to season
  • 1 tsp worcestershire sauce
  • a thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced
  • 1/2 tsp chinese five spice
  • Pinch of chili powder
  • 1 tsp white sugar(optional)
  • 375g ramen noodles
  • 400g sliced cooked meat of your choice
  • 2 tsp sesame oil

Steps to make ramen

  1. Mix the chicken stock, 3 halved garlic cloves, 4 tablespoons of say sauce, 1 teaspoon of Worcestershire sauce, a sliced thumb-sized piece of ginger, Â½ tsp Chinese five-spice, a pinch of chili powder and 300 ml water in a stockpot or large saucepan, bring to the boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 5 mins.
  2. Cook 375 grams of ramen noodles following the pack instructions, then drain and set aside
  3. Slice 400 grams of cooked pork or chicken, fry in 2 teaspoons of sesame oil until just starting to brown, then set aside.
  4. Divide the noodles between four bowls. Top each with a quarter of the meat, 25 grams of spinach, 1 tablespoon of sweetcorn, and two boiled egg halves each.
  5. Strain the stock into a clean pan, then bring to the boil once again.
  6. Divide the stock between the bowls, then sprinkle over 1 shredded nori sheet, sliced spring onions or shallots and a sprinkle of sesame seeds. Allow the spinach to wilt slightly before serving.

Enjoy!
